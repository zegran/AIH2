# WP5 figure catalog (v2 — Q1 re-design)

Generated by `src/analysis/figures/wp5_figures.py` (deterministic, seed 42). Shared style:
`src/analysis/figures/pubstyle.py` (Okabe–Ito palette, ≥7 pt fonts, vector PDF + 300 dpi PNG).
Every number traces to `data/curated/aih2_v1.csv`, `data/wp1/rate_extraction.csv`, or
`results/real_v1/metrics/*`. Encoding choices follow Cleveland–McGill (position/length over
area/angle/colour), Weissgerber (show the distribution, not a bare bar), and Jeong 2024 (3D only
for an intrinsically-3D surface, always with a 2D companion). Captions: writing-anti-ai discipline —
say what the reader should see, no embellishment.

## fig1_optimism_gap — leakage control
**Plots:** each model as a dumbbell from study-grouped R² (blue ●) to random-split R² (orange ■);
hollow diamond = grouped R² with the dominant study removed (tree models). Dashed line at R²=0.
**Encoding why:** a connected dot (dumbbell) puts the two CV estimates on a common position axis and
encodes the *collapse* as a length — more accurate than two adjacent bars (Cleveland–McGill). Legend
sits below the panel so no marker is occluded.
**Read:** trees reach R²≈0.55 under random splits but fall below 0 under study-grouped CV
(optimism gap 0.62–0.85); kNN falls to −1.5. **So:** naive pooling overstates generalization — the
control that motivates the rest of the paper. Foil: Das 2023 single-study in-sample ANN (R²≈0.998).
**Trace:** `metrics/cv_metrics.csv`, `metrics/porciuncula_sensitivity.csv`.

## fig2_variance_decomposition — HEADLINE
**Plots:** Cleveland dot plot of between-study (study-mean-yield) variance explained, R², one row per
predictor, sorted; 95% cluster-bootstrap CI as the bar. Physical regime (orange ■) vs method
covariates (blue ●); black ◆ = all method covariates jointly.
**Encoding why:** dot = point estimate (position), bar = CI (length) — the two most accurate channels;
regime vs method distinguished by colour *and* marker shape so it survives grayscale (no bare bars,
per Weissgerber). 1.5-column width.
**Read:** method covariates jointly explain 0.55 of between-study variance vs 0.02 for physical regime
(~27×); temperature-control method alone explains 0.33. **So:** the apparent contradictions are
predominantly methodological — associational, and the temperature-control effect survives control for
regime (incremental R² +0.47, CI [0.04, 0.77]). **Trace:** `TB_method_variance.md`,
`TB_confound_check.md`.

## fig3_particle_size_consistency — flagship contradiction, resolved
**Plots:** for each study that varies particle size, raw (D₅₀, yield) points (log x) plus a robust
Theil–Sen trend line; per-study Spearman ρ in the legend.
**Encoding why:** points show the actual within-study data (Weissgerber); the Theil–Sen line conveys
direction without implying a false sequence. Earlier connected "trajectories" were dropped because
replicate points share a D₅₀, so a connecting segment can slope the wrong way and misread.
**Read:** all 5/5 studies trend negative (ρ −0.29 … −0.87): smaller particle → higher yield within
each study. **So:** the "direction-changing" particle-size contradiction is internally consistent
within studies — a cross-study integration artifact, not a physical sign reversal. **Trace:**
`TA_within_study.md`, `aih2_v1.csv` (5 studies).

## fig4_ea_spread — apparent Eₐ does not organize by regime
**Plots:** strip plot of per-study apparent Eₐ (points) with a class-median bar per `system_class`;
literature band 3.5–102.6 kJ mol⁻¹ shaded; legend above the panel.
**Encoding why:** a strip plot shows every point and the full spread (Weissgerber) rather than a mean
± SE bar that would hide the within-class range. Median bar = robust centre.
**Read:** within-regime spread ≈ between-regime spread (liquid-metal-activated spans 8.5–58 kJ mol⁻¹).
**So:** the coarse regime label does not capture the kinetics; finer kinetic↔diffusion physics
(Saceleanu 2019) operates within studies — hence "predominantly", not "purely", methodological.
**Trace:** `rate_extraction.csv` (ea_kj_mol rows), `H3_arrhenius.md`.

## fig5_dataset_composition — data resource
**Plots:** horizontal bars of per-`system_class` yield-row counts (with study counts) + a quality-tier
stack (A/B/C); kinetic-table size noted.
**Encoding why:** counts are lengths on a common baseline — bars are exactly right here (no
distribution to show). Horizontal layout keeps class labels readable.
**Read:** 315 yield rows / 31 studies (3 classes ≥40 rows, 2 thin exploratory) + 76-row kinetic table.
**So:** a curated, provenance-tracked, QC'd open dataset (CC-BY) — a standalone contribution.
**Trace:** `aih2_v1.csv`, `rate_extraction.csv`.

## fig6_within_study_surface — within-study cleanliness vs cross-study chaos (justified 3D)
**Plots:** porciuncula2012 NaOH/foil factorial, yield over (temperature, NaOH concentration).
(a) 2D filled contour (primary, readable); (b) the same data as a 3D surface companion.
**Encoding why:** this is the one panel whose data are intrinsically a surface z = f(x, y) over two
continuous predictors, so 3D is justified (Jeong 2024) — and only here. It is always paired with the
2D contour as the primary readable panel; 3D ticks are thinned since the contour carries the values.
No other panel uses 3D; no 3D bars/pies anywhere.
**Read:** within this single study the response is a smooth, monotone surface (yield rises with both T
and concentration). **So:** within-study data are clean and well-behaved; the contradictions live in
the cross-study integration, not in any one experiment. **Trace:** `aih2_v1.csv` (porciuncula NaOH
foil, 4×5 grid).

**Caveats (all figures):** associational, not causal; n=31 studies / 3–9 per regime → nulls are
"no detectable effect at this n", not "no effect"; thin exploratory classes (waste_al, liquid_metal).
